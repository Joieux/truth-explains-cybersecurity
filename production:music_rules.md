# Background music rules for Truth Explains

Goal
Music should support the voice.
If you notice the music, it is too loud or too busy.

Music selection rules
Choose calm ambient or soft minimal beats.
Avoid vocals.
Avoid strong melodies that pull attention.
Avoid dramatic rises and drops.

One track per video
Use one track for the entire video whenever possible.
If you must change tracks, change once only and keep the mood the same.

Volume rule you can trust
Set voice first.
Then bring music up until you can barely tell it is there.
Then reduce it slightly.

Practical listening checks
Phone speaker check
If music competes with words on a phone, lower it.

One earbud check
If music grabs your focus in one earbud, lower it.

Quiet room check
If the music feels emotional or cinematic, swap it.

Duck rule
If you use automatic ducking, keep it gentle.
The goal is natural breathing room, not pumping.

Intro and outro rule
No music changes in the first 10 seconds.
No music swell at the end.
Stay calm all the way through.

Consistent brand sound
Pick 3 to 5 tracks that fit the channel.
Reuse them across videos.
Familiar sound builds trust.

Export checklist
Voice is always clear
No harsh high end
No bass that masks words
Music stays steady and quiet
