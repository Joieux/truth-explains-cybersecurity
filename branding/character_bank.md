## QUICK INSERTS (analogies and phrases — unchanged)

Everyday life
1. Front door logic — Lock it. Check first. Do not open up just because someone sounds confident.
2. Group chat energy — If the message feels off, pause. Ask a friend. Verify.
3. Extended warranty robocalls — Same scam pattern. Same volume. Not personal. Fishing.
4. Package delivery texts — If it is urgent and sloppy, it is usually a trap.

School and work
1. Integrity is the grade change — You did the work, but the record got altered.
2. Availability is the system outage — You need it now, but it is down.
3. Confidentiality is private notes — If the wrong people can see it, it is a problem.

Pop culture
1. Not a spy movie — No dramatic music. Most attacks are boring and repetitive.
2. Sci fi green code myth — Real attacks are social engineering and bad habits.
3. Supervillain button energy — If a link feels like a trap button, do not press it.

Cyber phrases translated
1. Phishing — A scam dressed up like something you trust.
2. Social engineering — Someone talking you into giving them access.
3. Malware — A harmful program you did not invite.
4. Ransomware — Your files get locked and they demand payment.
5. Two factor — A second lock for your digital front door.

Quick laugh lines
1. If an email is yelling at you, that is already suspicious.
2. Nobody needs your password that bad.
3. If it feels rushed, that is the scam working.
4. We are not aiming for perfect. We are aiming for harder to hack.
